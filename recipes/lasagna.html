Lasagna

Description

Lasagna (UK: /ləzænjə/,[1] US: /lənjə/, Italian: [laˈzaɲɲa]), also known as lasagne (Italian: [laˈzaɲɲe]), is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types,[2] made in very wide, flat sheets. The same-named Italian dish is made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such a s ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, m ozzarella and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices.[3] The dish may be topped with grated cheese, which bec omes melted during baking. Typically cooked pasta is assembled with the other ingredients and then baked in an o ven (al forno). The resulting baked pasta is cut into single-serving square or rectangular portions.
